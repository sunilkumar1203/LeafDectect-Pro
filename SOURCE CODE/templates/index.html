ABOUT PROJECT

Problem:

The tea industry plays a crucial role in the global economy, with tea leaves being a widely consumed beverage. However, the health and productivity of tea plants are often threatened by various diseases, including fungal, viral, and bacterial infections. Early and accurate detection of these diseases is essential for timely intervention and effective disease management. Manual inspection and diagnosis of diseased tea leaves can be time-consuming, subjective, and prone to errors. Hence, there is a need for an automated system that can assist in detecting and identifying diseases in tea leaves with high accuracy.

Solution:

In this project, we will be building a deep learning CNN model that can detect and classify types of Disease in Tea Leaves . A web application is integrated with the model, from where the user can upload a Leaf image and see the analyzed results on UserInterface.




WE CLASSIFY

ALGAL LEAF

Algal disease in tea leaves refers to the occurrence of algae on tea plants, which can negatively impact tea production and quality.

ANTHRACNOSE

Anthracnose disease in tea leaves is a fungal infection caused by the pathogen Colletotrichum sp. It manifests as dark, sunken lesions on the tea leaves, stems, and shoots.Anthracnose can lead to reduced tea quality and yield if not properly managed through cultural practices and fungicidal treatments.

BIRD EYE

Bird eye disease in tea leaves is a fungal infection caused by the pathogen Mycena citricolor. It manifests as small circular lesions with brown centers and yellowish halos, resembling bird's eyes.

BROWN BLIGHT

Brown blight disease in tea leaves is a fungal infection caused by Exobasidium vexans. It leads to the appearance of brown blisters or lesions on the leaf surface, often accompanied by leaf curling and distortion.

HEALTHY LEAF

Healthy tea leaves refer to leaves that are free from any visible signs of diseases, pests, or abnormalities. They exhibit vibrant green color, uniform texture, and lack any discoloration, spots, or deformities. Healthy tea leaves are essential for producing high-quality tea with optimal flavor, aroma, and nutritional value.

RED LEAF SPOT

Red leaf disease in tea leaves is a condition characterized by the appearance of red discoloration on tea plant foliage. It is caused by various factors, including nutrient deficiencies, viral infections, or physiological disorders.

WHITE SPOT

White disease in tea leaves, also known as powdery mildew, is a fungal infection caused by the pathogen Erysiphe polygoni. It manifests as a white powdery coating on the leaf surface, hindering photosynthesis and leading to leaf discoloration, reduced growth, and potential yield losses.

GRAY LIGHT

Grey disease in tea leaves is a fungal infection caused by Botrytis cinerea. It manifests as greyish-brown patches on the surface of tea leaves, affecting their quality and reducing tea production.
